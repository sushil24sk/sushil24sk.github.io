Labrador Retriever

The Labrador Retriever was bred to be both a friendly companion and a useful working dog breed. Historically, they earned their keep as a fisherman’s helper:

  • hauling nets
  • fetching ropes
  • fish from the chilly North Atlantic

Today’s Lab is as good-natured and hardworking as their ancestors, and they’re also America’s most popular breed.

Labs work as retrievers for

  • hunters
  • assistance dogs
  • show competitors
  • and search and rescue dogs, among other canine jobs.
